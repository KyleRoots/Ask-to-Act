#5 - Stakeholder Pitch Deck & Executive Summary
What & Why
Produce two deliverables that communicate the MCP connector business to non-technical business partner stakeholders (already part of the organization, not outside investors): (1) a polished slide deck presentation and (2) a plain-English executive summary. Both should reflect the fully refined business narrative, incorporating the Perplexity analysis and all strategic context from planning discussions.

The product name is TBD — use [PRODUCT NAME] as a placeholder throughout, or whichever name the user confirms before execution (leading candidates: Relay, Conduit, Switchboard). Do not reference Bullhorn or ChatGPT in the product name or tagline.

Done looks like
A slide deck artifact rendered in the browser, covering the full business narrative, designed for a non-technical internal business partner audience (10–15 minute walkthrough)
A multi-page executive summary (rendered as a clean web page with a print-friendly layout) written in plain English, covering what, how, money, and valuation — matching the four things internal partners need to hear
Both outputs are self-contained and can be presented without the author needing to explain context
Customer zero context (Myticas Consulting, ~20-person staffing firm using Bullhorn) is referenced as a concrete proof point, not as a market definition
Out of scope
Legal or financial projections requiring CPA/attorney input
Outside investor materials (cap table, term sheets, pitch competition formatting)
Product build or technical implementation
Context & Framing Notes
Audience: Internal business partners who are already bought in at the organization level — they need scope clarity, not persuasion from scratch. The tone is confident and direct, not a fundraising pitch.
The four things they need: (1) What we're doing and why, (2) How it works, (3) How we make money, (4) How this adds valuation if we sell the solution.
Market approach: All-market from day one — SMB through enterprise. The product architecture and pricing tiers support both without custom builds. Don't narrow the story to one segment.
Customer zero: Myticas Consulting (staffing, ~15–25 people, Bullhorn). Reference as validation that real-world use exists, not as a ceiling on ambition.
Competitive honesty: Acknowledge Bullhorn Amplify and native vendor tools. Position clearly: we work alongside or instead of them, we're model-agnostic, and we're cheaper per seat.
Tagline direction: "We sell the rails, not the chatbot." or similar infrastructure-first framing.
Steps
Set the narrative spine — Establish the story arc used across both deliverables, mapped to the four partner questions:

What & Why → Problem + Solution slides
How It Works → Mechanism slide (three-box diagram: AI model → Connector → ATS/CRM)
How We Make Money → Business model + pricing slide
Valuation / Exit → Market size + why this is sellable slide
Build the slide deck — Create a slides artifact. One idea per slide, minimal text, designed to be walked through verbally. Slides:

Cover: [PRODUCT NAME] + tagline
The Problem: recruiters (and any knowledge worker) copy-paste between AI tools and business systems daily — hours lost, no audit trail, no scale
The Solution: a universal connector layer that lets any AI model take action inside any business system via plain English
How It Works: three-box visual — User's AI (ChatGPT/Claude/etc.) → [PRODUCT NAME] Connector → Their System (Bullhorn, Salesforce, etc.)
The Moat: four pillars — (1) model-agnostic, (2) deep domain adapters per platform, (3) governance & audit as a product feature, (4) first-mover on MCP protocol becoming the industry standard
Competitive Landscape: native tools like Bullhorn Amplify vs. us — they lock you to one AI and one system; we connect any AI to any system
Business Model: base platform fee + per-connector fee + per-active-seat fee + one-time onboarding; enterprise compliance tier as premium
Market Opportunity: starts with ATS/recruiting, expands to any CRM, any vertical, any AI model — the TAM grows with AI adoption
Why Now: MCP is the emerging standard; enterprises are buying AI action layers now; first-mover positioning is available today
Cost to Scale: no AI model to train, no data to store, minimal infrastructure — small team, high margin, self-serve model
Proof Point: Myticas Consulting — real staffing firm, real Bullhorn environment, already in motion
Valuation / Exit: infrastructure and integration layers command strong multiples; comparable exits in the API/integration space; acquirers include ATS vendors, AI platforms, and enterprise software consolidators
What We Need: clear, direct statement of what the partners are being asked to align on (resources, timeline, go/no-go)
Build the executive summary — A clean, multi-section web page with a print-friendly layout. Sections:

One-paragraph plain-English description (repeatable by a non-technical person)
The problem, quantified (time lost per recruiter per week, cost of manual entry)
How it works, in three steps
Pricing model with worked examples:
10-seat firm: estimated ~$1,500–$2,000/month total
50-seat firm: estimated ~$4,500–$6,000/month total
100-seat firm: estimated ~$8,000–$12,000/month total
One-time onboarding: $2,500–$5,000
Enterprise compliance tier: add-on
Our cost to deliver (low infrastructure, no AI API costs on our end, lean team)
Competitive comparison table: [PRODUCT NAME] vs. Bullhorn Amplify vs. building in-house
Risks and mitigations (shows diligence — permissions/security, platform risk, "build vs. buy" objection)
The exit / valuation story: what makes this sellable and to whom
One-sentence pitch + elevator version
Style and polish — Professional, boardroom-ready design. Slide deck: clean typography, restrained color palette (suggest dark navy / white / one accent color), no decorative clip art. Executive summary: well-structured memo aesthetic — readable on screen, clean when printed.
