Short answer: **you do not have to be Enterprise**. Plus and Pro users also get MCP support via Developer Mode, but the way you’d package your product will differ between “individual” plans (Plus/Pro) and org plans (Business/Enterprise/Edu).[1][2]

***

## What ChatGPT Plus/Pro can do with MCP

OpenAI’s docs and public comms say that **ChatGPT Developer Mode provides full MCP client support** (read and write tools) and that it’s available in beta to **Pro, Plus, Business, Enterprise, and Education accounts** on the web.[2][3]

Key points for Plus/Pro:

- Eligibility: Developer Mode is explicitly listed as available to **Plus and Pro** users (along with Business/Enterprise/Edu).[3][4][2]
- Capabilities: In Developer Mode, users can add remote MCP servers, which means they can connect to *any* MCP server that exposes tools (including one you build, or third-party MCP servers like CData’s Bullhorn CRM MCP).[5][6][2]
- Actions: MCP tools can be read or write; OpenAI flags this as “powerful but dangerous,” and ChatGPT will require explicit confirmation for write actions.[2][3]

Practically, that means an individual recruiter with ChatGPT Plus/Pro can:

1. Enable Developer Mode in settings.  
2. Add your Bullhorn MCP server URL (or a third-party Bullhorn MCP, e.g. CData/StackOne/Zapier-style MCP endpoints).[6][7][8][5]
3. Use natural language in ChatGPT and have tools call Bullhorn to read/update data, subject to whatever constraints you define.

***

## When Enterprise/Business *does* matter

Where Enterprise (or at least Business) becomes important is not **basic connectivity**, but **governance and rollout at scale**:

- Business and Enterprise add shared workspaces, admin controls, SSO, RBAC, and more robust security/compliance features.[1]
- For org-level MCP apps (vs. one-off connectors in Developer Mode), admins can configure which tools are available, enforce org-wide policies, and manage access centrally.[9][10]

So for your product:

- **Yes**, you can support a “self‑serve” path for any recruiter with Plus/Pro:  
  “Have ChatGPT Plus/Pro? Enable Developer Mode, paste this MCP URL, and you’re connected to Bullhorn.”  
- But the **high-value, security-sensitive deployments** (larger agencies, corporate teams) will almost certainly want **Business or Enterprise** so their security/admin folks can control:  
  - Which MCP apps are allowed  
  - Which Bullhorn actions are exposed  
  - Who can use what, and with which permissions  
  - Logging and auditability

That maps nicely to your earlier vision: small/mid customers can get started on Plus/Pro with minimal friction; serious orgs buy your platform + Business/Enterprise to get real governance.

***

## Implications for your solution design

For your product strategy, this implies two tiers:

1. **Individual mode (Plus/Pro)**  
   - Target: power users, solo recruiters, small teams.  
   - Experience: “Bring your own ChatGPT Plus/Pro, flip on Developer Mode, add our MCP URL, and you’re talking to Bullhorn.”[4][2]
   - You handle the Bullhorn side (auth, tools, safety), they handle their own ChatGPT subscription.

2. **Org mode (Business/Enterprise/Edu)**  
   - Target: mid-size + enterprise agencies.  
   - Experience: admins configure the MCP app centrally, wire it to Bullhorn once, and then users just “click to connect” inside ChatGPT with appropriate guardrails.[10][9][1]
   - Your value prop is governance, templates, field mapping, and security posture, not just connectivity.

Given what you want—“I can fire up my ChatGPT or Claude account, click a button, it’s synced to my ATS end-to-end, and I explain what to do”—this is actually ideal: you can support **Plus/Pro users today via Developer Mode** and still build a more sophisticated story for Enterprise without changing your core architecture.[3][2]

Do you want to prioritize designing for the “individual power user on Plus/Pro” motion first, or are you leaning toward anchoring the product around Business/Enterprise-style centralized control from day one?
